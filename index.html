🔑 ErgoPass – Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 27, 2026

Developed by Dina N. Sharma

Overview

ErgoPass is a local password manager Chrome extension. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data ErgoPass handles and how.

Data We Collect

ErgoPass does not collect, transmit, or store any user data on external servers. All credentials and settings are stored exclusively on your own device using Chrome's built-in local storage, encrypted with AES-256-GCM encryption.

Data Stored Locally

The following data is stored only on your device and never leaves it:

  • Saved login credentials (usernames and passwords) — encrypted with AES-256-GCM
  • Your master password hash — stored securely, never in plain text
  • Your recovery email address — stored locally for OTP recovery purposes only

Third Party Services

The only external service ErgoPass uses is EmailJS (emailjs.com), which is used solely to send a one-time password (OTP) to your recovery email when you request a master password reset. Only the OTP code and your recovery email address are sent to EmailJS. No credentials, passwords, or vault data are ever shared with EmailJS or any other third party.

Data Sharing

ErgoPass does not sell, trade, or transfer any user data to third parties. We do not use your data for advertising, analytics, or any purpose other than providing the password manager functionality.

Security

All credentials are encrypted using AES-256-GCM with PBKDF2 key derivation (100,000 iterations) before being stored. This is the same encryption standard used by banks and governments worldwide.

Your Rights

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you have complete control over it at all times. You can delete all data at any time by removing the extension from Chrome.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.
